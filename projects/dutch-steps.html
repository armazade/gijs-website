The Dutch Steps

Housing Block

Project Info

  • Total Dwellings: 96
  • 6 Dwelling Types
  • Structure: CLT + Concrete

Dwelling Types

  • Studios: 57
  • Student Apartments: 9
  • Small Apartments: 3
  • Medium Apartments: 17
  • Large Apartments: 5
  • Row Houses: 5

Amenities

  • Exercise Room
  • Cinema
  • Study Library
  • Cafes (x2)
  • Workshops (x4)
  • Bike Storage
  • Community Gardens

The Dutch Steps is a housing block that aims to create a multi-generational, living-working environment. It incorporates student, starter, and family housing in a complex space that balances recreation, working, and consumer functions in order to allow these different parties to not only live together, but improve the quality of life of the community as whole.

In the development of this building, sustainability and circularity were prioritized by implementing measures such as dismountable and reusable concrete elements, prefabricated demountable construction elements, durable concrete composition, and inward balconies.

The building construction is primarily a CLT load-bearing structure, with the exception of the base which houses the parking garage and the elevator shafts  both made of concrete.

Design Concepts

  • Different Volumes
  • Road Shelter
  • Public Courtyard
  • Sunlight Optimization

Different Volumes: The building is split into three different volumes, each with a distinct shape and different living options.

Sheltered from Busy Road: The two big volumes on the north protect the courtyard and the rest of the neighborhood from the busy road.

Public Courtyard and Amenities: The building features a large public courtyard where interaction between inhabitants can take place and provides enough room for sunlight to enter.

Sunlight Optimization: The main building is stepped for ultimate sunlight exposure, as is the atrium in the biggest volume.
