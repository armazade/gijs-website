The Escape

Neighborhood Design

Project Info

  • Type: Neighborhood
  • Focus: Culinary Culture
  • Theme: Food & Nature

Key Features

  • Main Path Integration
  • Raised Pathway
  • Floating Farm Docks
  • Encapsulated Streets
  • Bay Windows
  • Community Squares

For this neighborhood, the goal was to restore the relationship we have with food and nature. Since industrialization, people have lost their connection to the food they are eating, becoming less aware of what they eat and where it comes from. To restore this relationship, the design organizes strong communities that adhere to the feeling of "midding."

Midding means: feeling the tranquil pleasure of being near a gathering but not quite in it, feeling blissfully invisible yet still fully included, safe in the knowledge that everyone is together and everyone is okay, with all the thrill of being there without the burden of having to be.

The neighborhood is meant to be a place where people can escape from their daily hassles and enjoy a receptive culinary cultural place, where fresh food comes directly from the land, thereby contributing to restoring the relationship we have with food.

Design Concepts

  • Main Path
  • Raised Pathway
  • Openings & Squares
  • Encapsulated Street

Main Path: The neighborhood is built on the cross-section of pathways leading to the harvest neighborhood and the research neighborhood.

Raised Pathway: To allow enough space for delivery vessels from the floating farms, the pathway is raised. Underneath, ships can dock and storage is placed.

Openings & Different Volumes: To break the linearity of the neighborhood, certain squares and openings are added. Here terraces can be placed or markets can be held.

Encapsulated Street: The main street is embraced by windows that curve above the street. In each bay window, there is part of the kitchen of the apartments on top. The result is a bustling culinary street in which the private and public spaces blend.

The street is lined with buildings of different height and depth to give variety in the streetscape and provide space for different accommodations. The final result is a picturesque culinary neighborhood  it has something for everyone and is scattered with many hidden surprises.
