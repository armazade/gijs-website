New Centrality

Museum / Library

Project Info

  • Typology: Museum/Library
  • Size: 15,000 m²
  • Location: Sliedrecht, Netherlands
  • Client: Nationaal Baggermuseum

Themes

  • Heritage
  • Community
  • Light

Centrality conveys the concept that specific areas within a city hold a central role in social and cultural importance. These pivotal locations transcend geography, serving as hubs where social, economic, and political activities intersect. The essence of centrality is twofold—both a cognitive process and a social phenomenon.

The new Baggerarea is remodeled through small interventions to create new social and cultural significance. The main intervention opens up the inner dyke to the public, making it accessible only by pedestrians—as the old dyke used to be.

New functions are added including a making street in the Rotterdam Steelworks BV shipyard with a Box-in-Box system accessible from both sides of the dyke. The old Dredging museum is converted into a hotel, while the current Wow-Keet becomes a wooden-clad pavilion for rest and coffee.

Design Approach

  • Architectural Quotation
  • Heritage Mirroring
  • Collective Memory

Inspirations

  • Old Church & Reformed Church
  • Sliedrecht Town Hall
  • Dredging Contractor Houses
  • Historic Dredging Ships

In visual arts, literature and music, mirroring old masters within a contemporary context is often equated with creating original work. This approach is applied in the New NBM (Nationaal Bagger Museum) by mirroring elements distinct to Sliedrecht.

Main inspiration is drawn from the monumental buildings shaped by the area's main power forces: the old church and reformed church, the town hall, and the old dredging contractor houses. The building's shape also references historic dredging ships. All these elements find their way into an agglomeration in the New NBM.

The entrance is positioned towards the public dike. Its round shape appears approachable and inviting from all sides. Above the entrance glows a large stained-glass window, clearly signalling the museum's purpose while creating a welcoming ambiance inside the main lobby.
